Todd Chrisley Proudly Shares a Picture of His Children on a Magazine Cover See His Sweet Message

Todd Chrisley is the proud father of 5, and the grandfather two adorable kids -- and his offspring are the apple of his eye.

The August cover of Troy Linger's "Brentwood Magazine" features sons Grayson and Chase, and daughter Savannah, and of course, seven-year-old Chloe Chrisley.

TODD CHRISLEY DOTING DAD

Chrisley, who has 5 children, shares son Kyle, 28, and daughter Lindsie, 30 with first wife, Teresa Terry; and with wife Julie Chrisley, he shares son Chase, 24, daughter Savannah, 22, and their youngest, Grayson, 14.

The Brentwood cover shows Chase, Savannah, and Grayson, accompanied by their niece Chloe looking relaxed, photographed against a magenta background. Chrisley captioned the snap:

"So proud of these kids and blessed to be their dad, thanks so much @brentwood_lifestyle for such an amazing article and cover."

The glamorous Savannah is wearing a tailored black suit and is holding a handful of festive black and white balloons, while Grayson and Chase are sitting down in front of a plate of delicious cupcakes. Chase is holding the smiling Chloe on his lap.

TALKING TO THE YOUNGER CHRISLEYS

Savannah and Chase are now the stars of their own "Chrisley Knows Best" spinoff, "Growing Up Chrisley," which debuted on April 2, 2019. The show follows Savannah and Chase as they embark on a journey from Nashville to Los Angeles in search of their own path.

THE SCANDALOUS CHRISLEYS

Recently, the family has been rocked by several scandals, including allegations of the existence of a sex-tape featuring Lindsey Chrisley. Additionally, Todd and Julie have been indicted by a federal grand jury in August 2019 on 12 counts of bank and wire fraud, and tax evasion -- all of which they deny.

Chrisley accused Lindsie and his ex-wife Teresa Terry of maliciously denouncing on trumped out charges, while Lindsie claims her father and brother Chase have been pressuring her with the aforementioned sex tape...

It all makes for riveting TV, and that's what keeps the fans glued to their screens.
