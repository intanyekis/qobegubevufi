Lil Nas X Issues Statement Following &quot;J Christ&quot; Backlash

Lil Nas X shares a message with fans after backlash regarding his latest music video “J Christ.” Taking to Instagram, the Georgia native detailed how criticism of his take on religion has impacted his outlook.

“I know I messed up really bad this time. I can act unbothered all I want, but it’s definitely taken a mental toll on me,” detailed the 24-year-old in the Monday (Jan. 15) upload. “I’m not some evil demon guy trying to destroy everybody’s values and stuff like that. That’s not me.”

He continued to explain, “When I did the artwork, I knew there would be some upset people simply because religion is a very sensitive topic for a lot of people. But I also didn’t mean to mock — this wasn’t a f**k you to the Christians. It was literally me saying I’m back like Jesus.”

In the “J Christ” video, Lil Nas X made use of impersonated versions of Barack Obama, Kanye West, Taylor Swift, Oprah Winfrey, Ed Sheeran, Mariah Carey, Dolly Parton, and Michael Jackson. Additionally, the scenery includes heavenly inspiration, a basketball game with a devil, and other Christian-inspired themes.

In the single’s cover art, the musician is tied to a cross.

“I know this isn’t going to be an immediate swift, everybody moves forward. But I do want my Christian fans to know that I am not against you,” shared the “Old Town Road” performer. “I was put on this earth to bring people closer together and promote love. That’s who I am.”
