Early Life

Oosthuizen was born on October 19, 1982, in Mossel Bay, South Africa. As an amateur, he has won several titles including 2000 World Junior Championship, 2001 All African Games, Transvaal Stroke Play Championship, 2002 Indian Amateur Open Championship, and Irish Amateur Open Championship.

Career

Oosthuizen turned pro in 2002 at the age of 19. He has won several professional tournaments on the Sunshine Tour including Vodacom Origins of Golf Tour, Dimension Data Pro-Am, Platinum Classic, and the Telkom PGA Championship.

In 2010, he entered the 2010 Open Championship ranked 54th in the Official World Golf Ranking. His popularity went through the roof after winning the Open Championship. With that win, he became the fourth man from South Africa to win the Claret Jug and moved to the 15th in the Official World Golf Ranking. In March 2010, he won his first European Tour at Open de Andalucia de Golf and the 2010 Masters Par 3 contest.

After the 2010 Open Championship, Oosthuizen played in several events. In 2011, he won his third European Tour title winning Africa Open in a playoff. He then successfully defended his title at the Africa Open in 2012 with a two-stroke victory over Tjaart Van der Walt.

Oosthuizen was the runner-up at the 2012 Masters Tournament. He was defeated by Bubba Watson on the second hole of a sudden-death playoff. A week later, he claimed his fifth European Tour title at the Maybank Malaysian Open.

In 2012, Oosthuizen reached the top 10 of the Official World Golf Ranking for the first time in his career. On January 13, 2013, he won the Volvo Golf Champions and retained the title again in 2014. With that win, he was placed fourth in the Official World Golf Ranking.

Oosthuizen finished a joint runner-up in the 2015 Open Championship. This was his second consecutive runner-up in a major championship. He was also a joint runner-up at the PGA Championship in 2017.
